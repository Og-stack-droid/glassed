Since the first sketch

Built by people obsessed with how light bends.

Graycie Glasses began in a single Lagos workshop with one belief: eyewear should feel like jewelry that happens to help you see. Every frame that carries our name passes through the same small atelier, where cutting, polishing, and finishing are still done by hand — slowly, on purpose.

We work with a small acetate house in Italy and a single lens lab that has cut glass for three generations. We could scale faster with less care. We choose not to.

01 — Cut

Block to Blank

Acetate sheets are cut into rough blanks, each one inspected for grain and clarity before it earns the Graycie name.

02 — Shape

Hand-Filed Form

Frames are filed and shaped against your face's geometry, not a generic mould — three rounds of fitting, minimum.

03 — Finish

Polish & Sign

A final hand-polish brings out the depth of the acetate, then the artisan signs the inside of the temple.
